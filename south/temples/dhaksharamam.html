Dhaksharamam Sri manikyamba sametha Bheemeshwara Swamy Temple

LOCATION: Draksharamam village of Konaseema region, East Godavari district, Andhra Pradesh

Introduction

Daksharamam Temple is located in Draksharamam village of Konaseema region, East Godavari district, Andhra Pradesh, surrounded by lush greenery, canals, and fertile lands formed by the Godavari River. This sacred town lies about 40 km from Rajahmundry and is well connected by road, making it one of the most spiritually significant Shaiva centers of coastal Andhra. The serene rural atmosphere, combined with ancient temple architecture, creates a powerful spiritual environment that immediately makes a devotee feel grounded and calm.

Temple Description

Daksharamam is one of the Pancharama Kshetras, the five most powerful temples dedicated to Lord Shiva in Andhra Pradesh, and here Shiva is worshipped as Sri Bhimeswara Swamy. According to ancient legends, this temple stands on the spot where a portion of Sati Devi’s body fell after her self-immolation, making it a sacred Shakti Peetha as well. What deeply moves me about Daksharamam is how it unites both Shaiva and Shakta traditions — Shiva is worshipped in the form of a massive crystal (spatika) lingam, and Goddess Parvati is revered as Manikyamba Devi. The temple’s towering gopuram, intricate stone carvings, and centuries-old inscriptions silently narrate stories of devotion, sacrifice, and divine grace.

Spiritual Significance

Spiritually, Daksharamam holds immense power because it is believed that Lord Shiva manifested here to pacify the destructive energy of Daksha Yagna, restoring cosmic balance. The Shiva Lingam here is unique — it is said to grow in size over time and is partially enclosed to control its immense energy. Devotees believe that praying here removes ego, pride, and ancestral karmic blocks, especially those related to family lineage and responsibilities. Worship at Daksharamam is considered highly beneficial for inner stability, marital harmony, and spiritual awakening, as the temple represents the divine union of Shiva (consciousness) and Shakti (energy).

Daily Rituals & Practices

Daily rituals at Daksharamam follow traditional Shaiva Agama practices, beginning with Suprabhata Seva and early morning Abhishekam using sacred substances like milk, honey, water, and sandalwood paste. This is followed by Alankara Seva, Archana, and Naivedyam, offered with Vedic chants that fill the temple with powerful vibrations. Special Abhishekams on Mondays, Pradosham days, Maha Shivaratri, and Karthika Masam draw thousands of devotees. The worship of Manikyamba Devi is performed with equal importance, emphasizing the balance of masculine and feminine divine energies.
